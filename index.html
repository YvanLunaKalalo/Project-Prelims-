About Us

The following information is about BCAS:

School Opened

The school opened in June 2000 with 19 selected students out of the 72 students who took the examination and with the founders as the first teachers together with Mrs. Miriam Simera-Ramos.

Logo

Mount Maculot, which faces it to the west, served as inspiration for the school's logo. The mountain stands for a difficulty that the school and its pupils must overcome. Getting to the top, succeeding, and enjoying the victory are the challenges. Another way that the challenge is described is to "climb the mountain to kiss the clouds." On the other hand, "climbing the mountain" refers to the school's efforts to give its students the finest learning environment possible. For the students, it entails pushing themselves to the absolute limit to attain academic achievement.

Vision-Mission

BCAS exists to bring about the most desirable outcomes from the experiences it provides the student and for his community and the larger world outside. In pursuit of its vision, Batangas College of Arts and Sciences, Inc. shall provide excellent education that is holistic, interdisciplinary, innovative and culture sensitive.

Academics

Elementary Department

It is usually where children are taught for the first six or sometimes eight years of their education. Paaralang Elementarya or elementary education is the first part of the educational system, and it includes the first six years of compulsory education from grade 1 to 6, with an optional 7th grade offered by some schools. Major subjects include maths, science, English, Filipino and social sciences.

High School Department

With the implementation of the K-12 program, BCAS opened a Senior High School (SHS) unit with the ABM, STEM, and HUMSS strands available. A high school is a secondary school, where teenagers are educated before starting college or getting jobs. Most high schools have four numbered grades, from ninth to twelfth. After middle school or junior high comes high school.
